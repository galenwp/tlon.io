We believe in the power of computers to connect people, but we’re tired of today’s internet. So we've been building a new kind of OS from scratch that lives in the cloud, called Urbit.

Urbit is simple, built to last forever and is 100% owned by its users. There are no ads, no tracking and no megacorp watching over you. You decide what you see, who you connect with and how you compute.

We want to make it easy for anyone to use Urbit in just a few clicks. Our hosting service does that, and we're almost ready to roll it out. If you'd like to try it, join the waitlist below.

Today, on the surface, Urbit is a calm, independent place for you and your friends to chat, write and share links. Under the hood, Urbit is a completely general-purpose computing platform and decentralized network that’s a new layer on top of the internet. Your Urbit data and identity are yours to keep, forever.

At Tlon, we find today's megacorp platforms to be frustrating to use, often creepy, and mostly disappointing. We wanted a way to build high-trust communities, to keep our data forever, and to explore what's possible when people control their own software.

We weren't satisfied solving this problem halfway - we wanted a lasting solution. So we started from the ground up.. Urbit makes it possible for an individual to control their own programs and data; Tlon makes it possible for anyone to use Urbit without ever touching the command line.

We don't want to juggle a mess of apps and we don't want to depend on giant platforms to make cloud computing possible. Urbit is the beginning of a new kind of personal cloud computing.

We'll happily let you know when more capacity is available.
